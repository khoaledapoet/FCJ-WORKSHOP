Feedback & Suggestions

Overall Evaluation of the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) Program

1. Work Environment
The learning and working environment at FCAJ is highly dynamic, professional, and technology-driven. The communication spaces (both online and via community platforms) are extremely open. Members of the AWS community are always ready to support, share resources, and answer questions, which greatly helped a Cloud newcomer like myself integrate and focus deeply on the tasks.

2. Support from Mentors / Team Admins
The support team is incredibly enthusiastic—a perfect 10. Even though Mr. Hung is busy, he always inspires everyone.

3. Alignment between Tasks and Academic Major
The Bootcamp was the perfect missing puzzle piece for my Computer Networks major. The theoretical knowledge of Subnets, IP Routing, and Firewalls learned at university was vividly actualized through hands-on provisioning of VPCs, Route Tables, and Security Groups. This experience successfully transformed my academic knowledge into practical Cloud deployment capabilities.

4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development
Through the PawVerse project, I significantly upgraded both my soft and hard skill sets. Technically, I mastered the deployment workflows for multi-tier cloud infrastructures, Cloud Operations (Cloud Ops), and cost management (FinOps). Regarding soft skills, I learned how to create professional system documentation and developed independent troubleshooting capabilities.

5. Culture & Team Spirit
The culture at FCAJ strongly encourages sharing and mutual respect. Technical discussions and knowledge-sharing sessions regarding infrastructure debugging brought everyone closer together. The AWS “Builders” spirit was highly evident: collaborating to build solutions and overcome technical blockers regardless of experience levels.

6. Policies / Benefits for Interns
The program provides an enterprise-grade practical environment, high-quality internal training materials, and, most importantly, the opportunity to interact directly with top-tier experts (Solution Architects) from AWS Vietnam. These are invaluable “benefits” for my early career development.

Additional Reflections

  • What were you most satisfied with during the internship?
    I am most proud of and satisfied with successfully transitioning the complex infrastructure architecture of the PawVerse project from logical diagrams to a live Production environment, fully integrating automated monitoring and multi-tier security pipelines.
  • What do you think the company needs to improve for future interns?
    Thought-out
  • Would you recommend interning here to your friends? Why?
    Absolutely. This is a phenomenal “launchpad” for any IT student, especially Computer Networks students looking to transition into the Cloud Computing domain.

Suggestions & Desires

  • Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?
    The program provided a complete, professional, and highly fulfilling experience. At this moment, I have no further recommendations for changes.
  • Would you like to continue with this program in the future?
    I am eager to continue engaging with the AWS community and hope to participate in more advanced training tracks (such as DevOps or AI/ML) in the future.
  • Other comments:
    Once again, my sincere thanks to the FCAJ organizing committee and my mentor, Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung, for creating such a meaningful and outstanding technological playground!
